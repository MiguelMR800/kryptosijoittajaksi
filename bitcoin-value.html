Why Does Bitcoin Have Value?

Bitcoin is not a company, nor is it backed by a central bank or government. Yet it has value – and many are willing to pay thousands of euros for it. Why?

Bitcoin's value stems from a combination of scarcity, decentralized technology, and community trust. New bitcoins are created at a fixed pace, with a maximum of 21 million ever in existence.

Moreover, Bitcoin runs without a central authority. It’s a transparent and open system where every transaction is publicly recorded on the blockchain. It cannot be manipulated, frozen, or stopped.
