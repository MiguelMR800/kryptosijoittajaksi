Hot Wallets Explained – MetaMask, Phantom, Jupiter and More

Hot wallets are crypto wallets connected to the internet – usually as browser extensions or mobile apps. They’re ideal for quick transactions, DeFi use, and NFT storage, but come with more risks compared to cold wallets.

Installation and Setup

  1. Download from an official source (App Store, Google Play, or Chrome Web Store)
  2. Create a new wallet and securely write down your seed phrase (12–24 words)
  3. Set a PIN code or password
  4. Add the necessary network (Ethereum, Solana, etc.)

How to Transfer Funds Between Exchange and Wallet

From Exchange to Wallet:

  • Copy your wallet address from the app
  • Go to your exchange (Binance, Bybit, etc.), open the withdrawal section
  • Paste your wallet address and choose the correct network
  • Confirm the transaction – incorrect network = funds lost!

From Wallet to Exchange:

  • Get your deposit address from the exchange
  • Paste the address into your wallet and send the funds
  • Always double-check everything before confirming

Tip: Always start with a small test transfer to be safe.

Security Tips

  • Never share your seed phrase
  • Only use official apps and extensions
  • Avoid storing large amounts in hot wallets
  • Consider a cold wallet for long-term storage
