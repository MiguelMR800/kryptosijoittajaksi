Trading Styles and Chart Patterns – Types, Signals and Probabilities

Trading Styles

  • Scalping – Very short-term trading (seconds to minutes). Targets tiny price movements, often repeated dozens of times per day.
  • Day trading – Positions opened and closed within the same day. No overnight holdings.
  • Swing trading – Trades held for days or weeks. Based on a mix of technical and fundamental analysis.
  • Position trading – Long-term speculative trading, positions can last for months. Based on macro trends and cycles.

Common Chart Patterns

Chart patterns are recurring formations in price action that may indicate the next likely move. Below are popular examples and their historical success rates:

1. Head & Shoulders

Signals a trend reversal. Three peaks with the middle being the highest. Success rate: ~80–85% for trend change.

2. Inverted Head & Shoulders

Bullish reversal after a downtrend. Three bottoms with the middle being the lowest. Success rate: ~80–85% upward breakout.

3. Double Top / Double Bottom

Double top signals resistance; double bottom signals support. Powerful reversal patterns. Success rate: ~75–80% reversal.

4. Symmetrical Triangle

Price consolidates in a tightening range. Neutral pattern, but often leads to strong breakout. Breakout probability: 60–70%.

5. Ascending / Descending Triangle

Ascending: horizontal resistance, rising lows – usually bullish.
Descending: horizontal support, falling highs – usually bearish.
Breakout probability: 70–75% in expected direction.

6. Rectangle

Sideways price movement between two horizontal levels. Indicates consolidation. Breakout probability: ~65%.

7. Ascending / Descending Channels

Sloped trend channels indicating rising or falling markets. A break outside the channel may signal trend continuation or reversal.

Summary

Technical analysis isn’t magic, but helps identify probability-based setups. Always combine patterns with indicators like volume or RSI, and never forget risk management!
